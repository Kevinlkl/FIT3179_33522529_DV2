The Economy of Malaysia

How is our economy?

Leading index of economic activity (LEI) is an index that combines several economic indicators to provide insights into the future while lagging index of economic activity (LAG) reflects past economic performance. Coincident index of economic activity (CEI) measures current economic conditions.

Key patterns to note:

  • All 3 indices show a general upward trend over the past decades.
  • LEI indicates positive future economic activity.
  • CEI reflects very positive current economic conditions.

Discussing Productivity by Sector

Productivity output per hour is normalized to a 2015 baseline (2015 = 100), allowing apples-to-apples comparison across sectors. Values above 100 indicate productivity gains relative to 2015, while values below 100 indicate declines.

Key patterns to note:

  • Manufacturing shows strong recovery post-2023 after a pandemic dip.
  • Services sustain steady improvements with fewer swings year-to-year.
  • Agriculture remains relatively flat compared to other sectors.

What roles do seaports play?

Malaysia maintains a relatively good trade balance with major sectors like Manufacturing and Food being export heavy while sectors like Fuel and Ores and Metals leaning towards imports. These dynamics highlight the importance of seaports in facilitating trade.

Key patterns to note:

  • Seaports like Pelabuhan Kelang and Pelabuhan Pasir Gudang facilitate high cargo traffic
  • Many seaports have access to crucial shipping lanes like Malacca and Tebrau Strait

Let's not ignore the airports!

Malaysia has many airports around the country, with major ones like Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA) and Kota Kinabalu International Airport,

What do they do?

  • Facilitate cargo transport both interstate and internationally
  • Support tourism and business travel, boosting the economy and attracting investors that boost trade.
  • Provide essential connectivity for remote areas.

How does good trade affect the nation?

Malaysia's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and Gross National Income (GNI) have shown consistent growth over the years, reflecting a robust economy boosted by trade and great productivity. Latest statistics in 2023 indicate great employment ratio by state as well.

Key patterns to note:

  • A majority of states have an employment rate of over 60%.
  • Selangor state has employment ratio of over 70% which is amazing when compared to international benchmarks.

How is the gender employment discrepancy?

when observing gender employment ratios across various states in Malaysia from 1992 to 2023, there are noticeable differences in employment rates where certain states exhibit significant decrease in gender employment gap.

Key patterns to note:

  • Selangor, Putrajaya and Kuala Lumpur display historical lows in gender employment ratios.
  • Shows job opportunities for women are increasing, thus forming a fairer job market.
